# 🌦️ Bangladesh Climate Change Analysis

> A comprehensive Data Science study of **123 years** of temperature and rainfall trends in Bangladesh (1901–2023), combining statistical analysis, machine learning, and time-series forecasting.

---

## 📌 Project Overview

Bangladesh is one of the most climate-vulnerable countries in the world. This project investigates long-term climate patterns using historical monthly temperature and rainfall data, uncovering evidence of climate change through data science techniques.

---

## 📊 Key Findings

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| 📅 Years Covered | 123 years (1901–2023) |
| 🌡️ Average Temperature | 25.07 °C |
| 🌧️ Average Annual Rainfall | 2,430.51 mm |
| 📈 Warming Rate | ~0.05 °C per decade |
| 🔥 Hottest Year | 2009 |
| 💧 Wettest Year | 2007 |
| ⚠️ Extreme Climate Years | 10 |

---

## 🔍 Analysis Includes

- **Climate Change Trends** — Long-term warming detection over 120+ years
- **Temperature Anomalies** — Decade-by-decade deviation from baseline
- **Rainfall Variability** — Identifying wet/dry extremes and seasonal patterns
- **Seasonal Analysis** — Monthly and seasonal climate cycles
- **Extreme Weather Events** — Flagging statistically significant climate events
- **Statistical Testing** — Trend significance using SciPy stats
- **Machine Learning** — Linear Regression for temperature prediction
- **Time-Series Forecasting** — Future temperature projection
